## Summary

A detailed review of how muscles in ME/CFS fail — they receive too little blood, build up harmful levels of sodium and calcium, produce too much oxidative stress, and lose their normal ability to contract — and how these changes might be detected by standard tests.

_(Full text in `paper.txt`; the summary above is the curated plain-language abstract — deepen from the full text when reading.)_

## Why this matters

Integrates molecular, cellular, and clinical evidence into a single picture of why ME/CFS muscles don't work normally, and proposes markers that could be used for diagnosis — supporting the muscle-focused treatment approach being pursued by researchers including Wirth and Scheibenbogen.
