## Summary

Reviews evidence that ME/CFS disturbs the brain's chemical messengers — the signals nerve cells use to talk to each other — which may explain symptoms like brain fog and feeling 'wired but tired'.

## Why this matters

From the two leading German ME/CFS researchers: it links the disease's effects in the muscles to its effects in the brain, reinforcing the case for the treatments they are developing.
