## Summary

Looked at tiny particles that cells shed into the blood (extracellular vesicles) in 49 ME/CFS patients versus 50 healthy people. Patients had more of these particles overall, and a handful of the proteins they carried differed, though none of those individual differences held up after strict statistical checking.

## Why this matters

The largest such extracellular-vesicle study in ME/CFS so far; a mostly exploratory result, but it adds a new (Norwegian) patient group to this growing biomarker approach, alongside similar work already in this database from the Scheibenbogen group.
