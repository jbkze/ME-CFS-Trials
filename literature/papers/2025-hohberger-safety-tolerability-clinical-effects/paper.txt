Summary
Background Rovunaptabin neutralises functional autoantibodies targeting G-Protein coupled receptors (GPCR-
fAAbs), observed in patients with Post-COVID syndrome. As we hypothesise an improvement of PCS by 
rovunaptabin, the aim of reCOVer was to investigate safety, tolerability, and clinical effects of rovunaptabin in 
PCS patients.
Methods reCOVer is a prospective, exploratory, placebo-controlled, double-blind, randomised phase IIa clinical 
investigator initiated trial with 1350 mg rovunaptabin with additional cross-over at the UniversitГӨtsklinikum 
Erlangen, Germany. The trial was registered in EudraCT, 2022-001781-35. Screening was done between 
21вӢ…11вӢ…2023 and 25вӢ…06вӢ…2024. Eligible participants (18вҖ“80 years) showed GPCR-fAAbs, at least 3/8 defined PCS 
symptoms persisting вүҘ3 months after COVID-19 and fatigue as major symptom. Participants were randomly 
assigned (1:1) to either receive rovunaptabin or placebo at day 0 (d0) and d48 with a follow-up of 28 days, 
respectively. Primary endpoint was the number of treatment emergent adverse events (TEAE) at d28 (co-primary 
endpoint: TEAE at d70); secondary endpoint focused on fatigue and quality of life.
Findings Thirty PCS patients were randomised and analysed. RCT analysis showed nine (rovunaptabin) and five 
TEAEs (placebo), yet without statistically significance (p = 0вӢ…1299; CI вҲ’14вӢ…80%; 63вӢ…02%); one serious adverse event, 
not related to treatment, was recorded. Rovunaptabin showed a neutralisation of GPCR-fAAb and a significant 
improvement of FACIT Fatigue Scale (effect size = 2вӢ…10, p = 0вӢ…0378), Bell score (effect size = 3вӢ…64, p = 0вӢ…0004), 
Fatigue Severity Scale (effect size = вҲ’2вӢ…66, p = 0вӢ…0088), and quality of life (4/8 items).

Research in context
Evidence before this study
Post-COVID syndrome, also known as post-acute sequelae of 
SARS-CoV-2 (PASC) or Long-COVID (LC), is defined as the 
persistence of clinical symptoms longer than three months 
after acute COVID-19. Currently, PCS therapy is based on a 
multidisciplinary approach with symptomatic, yet not causal 
treatment. Recent data suggest that PCS encompasses 
different subgroups, each of them requiring a specific causal 
therapy. One of those subgroups is defined by an 
autoimmune phenotype with a seropositivity of functional 
autoantibodies targeting G-protein coupled receptors (GPCR-
fAAbs). The aptamer rovunaptabin (BC007) has been shown 
to neutralise such GPCR-fAAbs in-vitro and in-vivo, therefore 
offering a causal therapeutic potential for PCS patients with 
GPCR-fAAbs. We searched PubMed from inception until 
December 12th, 2024, for articles with the terms вҖңBC007вҖқ, 
вҖңBC 007вҖқ, вҖңrovunaptabinвҖқ, вҖңPost-COVID RCTвҖқ, and вҖңPASC 
RCTвҖқ with no language restriction. This identified 53 articles, 
only one of which dealt with the use of rovunaptabin in PCS 
patients in the form of an individual healing attempt of a 
single PCS patient, performed by our group. Another clinical 
phase II trial investigating the treatment of PCS patients with 
rovunaptabin (BLOC, ID: NCT05911009) has been registered
in the database clinicaltrials.gov, however no publication has 
been issued yet.
Added value of this study
The reCOVer study is the first prospective, exploratory, 
placebo-controlled, double-blind, randomised phase IIa 
clinical investigator-initiated trial (IIT) of rovunaptabin 
(BC007) with additional cross-over in patients with Post-
COVID syndrome. This is the first investigator initiated 
clinical study to investigate rovunaptabin within this 
population. Rovunaptabin showed a temporary expected 
anticoagulatory effect with a good safety profile. Using 
complementary patient-reported outcome measurements, 
rovunaptabin showed a significant improvement of fatigue 
and its severity and quality of life in PCS patients compared 
to placebo.
Implications of all the available evidence
Due to the high disease burden and the yet unmet need of a 
causal therapy for PCS patients, the data of reCOVer indicate 
that rovunaptabin might offer a novel therapeutic option for 
an autoimmune subgroup of PCS patients. The data of the 
present study provides support for further clinical studies in a 
larger patient group with a long-term follow-up.

Trial design

A total of 37 PCS patients were selected for 
screening according to the inclusion and exclusion 
criteria by the investigators. Adult female and male 
patients, between ages of 18вҖ“80 years were eligible for 
inclusion. Previous SARS-CoV-2 infection had to be 
documented by a positive polymerase chain reaction 
(PCR) test. PCS symptoms had to persist at least three 
months prior to study inclusion, with a clinically rele-
vant fatigue (Bell score вүӨ 60) and at least three of the 
following symptoms: exercise intolerance, exertional 
dyspnoea, concentration disorders, brain fog, fatigue, 
unsteady gait, disorders of the sense of taste and/or 
smell, headaches. Furthermore, the presence of GPCR-
fAAbs was a prerequisite for participation. Exclusion 
criteria were: the therapeutic use of anticoagulants that 
could interact with the investigational medicinal prod-
uct (IMP) rovunaptabin and increase the tendency to 
bleed in the last two weeks before the screening visit, 
signs of long-term COVID-19 induced organ damage; a 
history of other known illnesses that could be cause for 
fatigue and reduced resilience (e.g., multiple sclerosis, 
anaemia); a history of obstructive sleep apnoea syn-
drome; obesity with a Body-Mass-Index вүҘ 30 kg/m 2 ,
acute and severe organ damage to the heart and lungs, 
malignancies or remission of malignant diseases 
within the last two years. No intake of interfering drugs 
that could lead to fatigue (e.g., opiates, antihistamines, 
antidepressants, NMDA antagonists) was permitted. 
Pregnant and breastfeeding persons were excluded. 
Furthermore, eye diseases with impairment of the 
retinal microcirculation, systemic diseases with eye 
involvement and glaucoma were excluded as reCOVer 
was accompanied by additive eye-based diagnostic 
measurements (e.g., OCT-angiography) for visual-
isation of capillary microcirculation, flow cytometry for 
measurements of the physics of blood cells, 7 T MRT

A total of 30 patients were included in the study and 
randomised (1:1). The duration of participation was 91 
days per participant according to the study protocol. 
The cross-over design was chosen to increase patientsвҖҷ
participation in the study (i.e., each participant will 
receive rovunaptabin). As no causal therapy is available 
in PCS treatment, no specific washout phase of other 
drugs was required prior to starting the trial (comor-
bidities can be seen in Suppl. Tables S1 and S2, con-
current medication in Suppl. Table S3). For this initial 
exploratory pilot study, a sample size of n = 30 was 
considered sufficient, to obtain data for a subsequent

Randomisation and masking
The randomisation sequence was generated by the 
statistician before starting the enrolment with the 
maximal procedure with software R 4вӢ…3 0, a 1:1 ratio 
and a maximum tolerated imbalance of 6. 
14 The length 
of the randomisation sequence was 40 to account for 
possible screening failures. The study was performed in 
a double-blinded manner, where participants and in-
vestigators were blinded. Unblinded members of the

the trial, as no medical emergency occurred. Unblinded

Safety
For analysis of safety and tolerability adverse events 
(AE) and vital signs (e.g., blood pressure) were recorded 
during the whole observation period. Haematology, 
blood-chemistry and coagulation tests were performed 
at all study visits. A physical examination was done on 
both treatment days (d0/d42), d1, d28, d43, and d70. 
Occurrence of AEs, adverse reaction (ARs), serious 
adverse event (SAEs) and serious adverse reaction 
(SARs) were recorded during the entire observation

Outcomes
Primary endpoint of the RCT was the occurrence of 
self-reported in face to face treatment-emergent adverse 
events (TEAEs) in the period from V2 (after adminis-
tration of rovunaptabin) to d28 compared between 
treatment and placebo group. Secondary endpoints 
were the treatment effect of rovunaptabin on self-
reported fatigue and self-reported quality of life, 
assessed by different scores: the Functional Assessment 
of Chronic Illness Therapy (FACIT) Fatigue Scale and 
Chalder Fatigue Scale (for fatigue), the Bell scale and 
Fatigue Severity Scale (FSS) for severity of fatigue, the 
Canadian Criteria (CCC, for the presence of myalgic 
encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/ 
CFS)), SF-36 questionnaire (for quality of life), DePaul
Symptom Questionnaire - Post-Exertional Malaise short 
form 8 (DSQ-PEM) and 6 min walking test (6MWT, 
Borg scales). The reply of each questionnaire was done 
individually from each participant locally at each visit 
and collected by blinded persons of the study team. 
The FACIT-Fatigue Scale is a self-reported 13-item 
questionnaire, assessing fatigue and its impact on 
daily activities over the past seven days. It is rated on a 
four-point Likert scale (4 = вҖңnot tired at allвҖқ to 0 = вҖңvery 
tiredвҖқ). Rating is scored from 0 to 52 (healthy). The 11-
item Chalder Fatigue Scale (CFS) assesses physical fa-
tigue (questions 1вҖ“7) and mental fatigue (questions 
8вҖ“11), scored with four response options ranging from 
вҖҳlessвҖҷ to вҖҳmuch moreвҖҷ. Rating is scored from 0 (healthy) 
to 33. The Bell Scale assesses fatigue severity, 
comprising a total of 10 responses with a rating from
0 to 100 (healthy). The 9-item FSS scored from 1 
(вҖңdisagreeвҖқ) to 7 (вҖңcompletely agreeвҖқ). The lower scores, 
the healthier the participant is. CCC comprises seven 
categories, including fatigue, PEM, sleep dysfunction, 
pain neurological and cognitive, autonomic, neuroen-
docrine and immune manifestations for a binary diag-
nosis of ME/CFS. The 5-item DSQ-PEM is scored from
0 (not present) to severe (including grading from 1 
(mild) to 4 (very severe)); additional five items were 
recorded in a binary and time-coded score. Quality of 
life was assessed by the 36-item Short Form (SF-36), 
including eight subscales, the physical component 
summary (PCS) and the mental component summary 
(MCS). Rating is scored from 0 to 100 (healthy). 6MWT 
registers walking distance in a self-determined speed 
within 6 min; data for persons, which were unable to 
perform the 6MWT, were excluded from this analysis. 
BORG rating of perceived exertion scale assessed the 
subjectively perceived exertion during 6MWT, graded 
from 6 (no exertion) to 20 (maximum exertion). BORG-
dyspnoea-scale assessed the subjectively perceived 
dyspnoea with a scoring from 0 (no) to 10 (maximum). 
Fatigue scores and quality of life were assessed at all 
visits from d0 to d91 (except at V1, V9). Six minute walk 
test (6MWT) was performed at d0, d7, d28, d42, d49, 
d70, and d91. Additional analysis of the within-
participant differences (part 2: cross-over) was con-
ducted after RCT for the co-primary endpoint, i.e., the 
occurrence of TEAEs from baseline (after administra-
tion of rovunaptabin) to d70. Data on race and ethnicity 
were not collected this was not the focus of the present 
study.

Statistical analysis
The statistical analysis was done by the software R. 
16 
The statistical analysis was pre-specified in a statistical 
analysis plan, which was finalised before the start of the 
enrolment. The statistical analysis was based on a fre-
quentist approach; significance level was set to 5% and 
tests and confidence intervals were two-sided. The study 
design was exploratory. For each analysis the popula-
tion were those patients, who provided data for this 
analysis. Missing values were not imputed. Thus, each 
fatigue score was tested separately without correction 
for multiple testing, as we did not know which of the 
endpoint would show a potential effect after treatment. 
The primary variable was the relative frequency of pa-
tients with at least one TEAE in the period from base-
line to d28. This frequency is calculated for both 
treatment groups and a 95%-confidence interval for the 
difference of these two frequencies is estimated. 
17 Also, 
the rate of TEAE per day was calculated and compared 
between the two treatment groups by a Poisson-test. 
The same methods were applied to the co-primary 
variable (TEAE from baseline to d70). Considering the 
different baseline values, baseline variable was a co-
variate. So, the models were adjusted for these imbal-
ances. For all other variables the data analysis was split 
in two parts: data acquired RCT at visit 8 and for all data 
up to visit 13. RCT analysis was done by linear mixed 
models adjusted for the baseline variable. The linear 
mixed models with random effects for repeated mea-
surements were used to compare difference between 
groups at d0, d28, and d70. We estimated the treatment 
effects between rovunaptabin and placebo for RCT data 
up to d28; for the cross-over data we used cross-over 
differences. Statistical methods for cross-over studies 
are described in Senn et al.

Results
Screening of the first patient was done on 22nd 
November 2023, screening of the last patient was 
screened on 25th June 2024. The final study visit was on 
10th September 2024; the study protocol remained 
unchanged during the course of the study. 37 patients 
with PCS were screened and assessed for eligibility, of 
whom 30 could be recruited for participation (Fig. 1). 
Reasons for exclusion were declined participation and 
withdrawal of consent (n = 4), not fulfilling either the 
inclusion or exclusion criteria due to facts occurring 
after screening (n = 1), noncompliance (n = 1) or due to 
a seronegativity for GPCR-fAAbs (n = 1).
30 participants were randomly assigned to the RCT 
with one rovunaptabin arm (n = 14) and one placebo 
arm (n = 16). Additionally, a cross-over treatment was 
conducted afterwards (Suppl. Figure S2). One partici-
pant, assigned to treatment, was withdrawn due to 
acute SARS-CoV-2 infection (i.e., exclusion criteria, 
drop out) after d28. 29 participants completed the trial. 
Of all participantsвҖҷ visits, five visits of three participants 
were not realised due to the patientsвҖҷ malaise (n = 3) or 
illness (n = 2). Therefore, 30 participants completed V7, 
including the complete data set for primary outcome.

RCT

mild or moderate severity, not related to treatment, as

number of TEAEs was 9 in participants receiving 
rovunaptabin and 5 in participants receiving placebo 
until d28, yet this difference was not significant

Discussion
The reCOVer study was an investigator-initiated, pla-
cebo-controlled randomised clinical phase IIa trial with 
additional cross-over to investigate safety, tolerability 
and clinical effects of rovunaptabin on fatigue, severity 
of fatigue and quality of life in PCS patients, showing a 
seropositivity for GPCR-fAAbs. Rovunaptabin has been 
shown to neutralise GPCR-fAAbs, both in-vitro and in-
vivo. 
19,20 Data published by us and others suggest an 
involvement of GPCR-fAAbs in the pathogenesis of 
PCS, leading to the hypothesis that neutralisation of 
such GPCR-fAAbs would improve PCS symptoms. 
21 An 
individual treatment of a PCS patient performed by our 
group, showed neutralisation of GPCR-fAAbs with a 
consecutive improvement of PCS symptoms after 
rovunaptabin treatment. 
15 The results of reCOVer 
confirmed these clinical observations: a significant 
improvement of fatigue and quality of life observed 
after infusion of rovunaptabin. As an intention-to-treat 
analysis (ITT) was performed including all rando-
mised patients, an underestimation of the treatment

can be assumed that a subset of GPCR-AAb mediate an 
additional functional activity (GPCR-fAAbs). The 
continuous binding of GPCR-fAAbs to their receptors 
stimulates the receptors due to their agonistic effect, 
decreases the number of receptors on the cell surface 
and their mRNA levels, 
25 consequently affecting e.g.,

26
Although only a single infusion of rovunaptabin 
was applied, the patients experienced a prolonged 
improvement of their symptoms. As rovunaptabin has 
only a short serum half-life of about 9вӢ…9 min, 
12 we 
hypothesise that the drug not only prevents binding of 
the GPCR-fAAbs to their receptors, but in addition, it 
likely exerts an immunomodulating effect on B-cells 
and/or plasma cells. Further research is needed to 
delineate this assumed interaction of rovunaptabin.
Of interest a further participantsвҖҷ subgroup showed 
a disappearance of GPCR-fAAbs without rovunaptabin 
treatment, but without an improvement of fatigue in 
the following weeks, compared to an improvement of 
fatigue in those participants, whose GPCR-fAAbs were 
neutralised after rovunaptabin. We assume that low 
serum levels of highly active GPCR-fAAb still might have 
a functional pathogenic effect, even when their concen-
trations drop below the detection limit of the bioassay. 
The method of screening for GPCR-fAAbs is based on a 
CM-bioassay. This laboratory method counts the differ-
ence of the beating rate of CMs after add-on of purified 
patientsвҖҷ sera compared to basal beating rate. This dif-
ference of the beating rate is transferred to a binary 
outcome: seropositivity of GPCR-fAAbвҖ”yes/no. As the 
CM-bioassay is a very labour and time intensive test, the 
test was done without determination of the titre. 
Current treatment trends in PCS emphasise a 
multidisciplinary approach, involving symptomatic, 
supportive, and rehabilitative care, with a significant 
role for patient self-monitoring and active participation 
in their recovery. Due to the severe difference in 
symptoms and severity, it is likely that the cause of PCS 
is multifactorial, summarising several pathogenetic 
molecular pathways. The present study focus on a 
specific group of patients with PCS with fatigue as 
major symptom and a seropositivity of GPCR-fAAb. 
The data of the present study showed that rovunapta-
bin neutralised GPCR-fAAb, followed by a clinical 
improvement of fatigue, the present findings may help 
to develop a potential novel causal treatment for this 
PCS group.
Different clinical trials with PCS patients are 
running, differing in study protocols, patientsвҖҷ charac-
teristics and outcome variables. 
27 To the best of our 
knowledge this is the first study of the application of 
rovunaptabin in PCS patients seropositive for GPCR-
fAAbs. The improvement of fatigue was apparent by 
the FACIT fatigue scale, Bell scale, FSS, and CCC, yet 
not by the Chalder fatigue scale, DSQ-PEM, and Borg 
scales. This data demonstrates clear differences be-
tween the different fatigue questionnaires. In addition, 
patients did not show a significant increase of their 
walking distance, as measured by the 6MWT.

thrombin binding aptamer 
10 with a mild-to-moderate

previous studies.

ReCOVer is the first RCT of rovunaptabin in PCS 
patients with a seropositivity of GPCR-fAAb, showing 
encouraging results. The additional cross-over design 
enabled analysis of inter-patientsвҖҷ differences in vali-
dated self-reported patientsвҖҷ outcomes. As no epide-
miology data on the presence of GPCR-fAAbs are 
available up to date, the number of participants was 
low, the study cohort represent an European cohort 
and recruitment was random as patients registered 
actively for a potential study participation, the data of 
the present study cannot be generalised. Further 
limitations were the use of self-reported question-
naires only, and the lack of established biomarkers 
except for GPCR-fAAbs. Patients had to complete a 
larger number of questionnaires, as the effect of 
rovunaptabin on the outcome variables was not 
known. As the follow-up duration was limited to a 
maximum of 91 days, the present results of reCOVer 
encourage a subsequent clinical phase II b, investi-
gating the doses and the effects in a larger patientsвҖҷ
group over a longer follow-up period, as well as sub-
sequent phase III trial.
The prevalence of PCS seems to be linked to several 
risk factors and even the number of re-infections, 
31 
thus, it can be assumed that the total number of pa-
tients with PCS will still increase in future, affecting 
individual life, public health care system and econ-
omy. 
32,33 The clinical trial reCOVer is the first RCT of 
rovunaptabin in PCS patients seropositive for GPCR-
fAAbs, showing a neutralisation of GPCR-fAAbs, fol-
lowed by a significant improvement of fatigue, severity 
of fatigue and quality of life compared to placebo. Next 
to an expected temporal anticoagulatory effect directly 
after treatment, participants of the rovunaptabin group 
showed mild to moderate TEAEs, compared to the 
placebo group, yet neither with a statistical significance, 
nor with treatment related SAEs. The data of the pre-
sent clinical trial suggest that rovunaptabin might offer 
a novel approach for a curative and causal therapy for 
an autoimmune subgroup of patients with PCS.
