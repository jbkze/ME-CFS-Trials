## Summary

Found that immune cells from ME/CFS patients overreact to signals from bacteria and viruses — producing far more inflammation-driving molecules than healthy controls — and that this overreaction is linked to disrupted energy production and gut bacteria imbalances.

_(Full text in `paper.txt`; the summary above is the curated plain-language abstract — deepen from the full text when reading.)_

## Why this matters

A large multi-institutional study (led by Ian Lipkin at Columbia) that pins down overactive 'first-response' immunity as a key driver of ME/CFS symptoms, and identifies several existing drugs — including metformin and rapamycin — as possible treatments to test.
