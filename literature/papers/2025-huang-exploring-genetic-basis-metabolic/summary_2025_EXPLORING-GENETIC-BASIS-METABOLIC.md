## Summary

Using UK Biobank data from nearly 37,000 people, researchers linked specific gene variants to blood chemistry abnormalities in ME/CFS patients, pointing to disrupted fat metabolism and neurotransmitter (brain chemical) pathways as genetically influenced features of the disease.

## Why this matters

A genome-wide analysis of ME/CFS metabolism in one of the world's largest health datasets — the genetic connections it finds could point to drug targets and help explain why patients' cells behave differently from healthy people.

## Notes

iScience 2025, published Dec 3 2025. PMC12796752. UK Biobank: 875 ME/CFS patients vs 36,033 controls. Found 112 significant SNP-metabolite associations specific to ME/CFS, mapping to HSD11B1 and SCGN genes (lipid metabolism, neurotransmitter transport, inflammation pathways).
