## Summary

Reviews how new technologies that can read thousands of gene and protein signals at once are being used to sort ME/CFS patients into distinct biological groups — defined by what is going wrong in their bodies — rather than by symptoms alone, using artificial intelligence to find the patterns.

## Why this matters

Lays out a roadmap for 'precision medicine' in ME/CFS — where each patient subgroup receives treatment matched to their specific biology — and identifies overactive innate immunity, exhausted immune T cells, and cellular energy failure as the likeliest targets for the first such treatments. From leading US ME/CFS researcher Nancy Klimas's group.

## Notes

PMID 42196410. Int J Mol Sci 27(10):4436. Published May 15, 2026. Frank (Nova Southeastern University, Klimas group) et al. Comprehensive review covering multi-omics (transcriptomics, proteomics, metabolomics), machine learning diagnostics (monocyte subsets, cell-free RNA), and precision medicine approaches; identifies innate immune activation, CD8+ T cell exhaustion with TOX/EOMES upregulation, and mitochondrial dysfunction as key molecular signatures.
