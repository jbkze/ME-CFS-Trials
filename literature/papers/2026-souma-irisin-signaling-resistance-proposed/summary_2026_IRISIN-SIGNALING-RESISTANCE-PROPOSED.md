## Summary

ME patients had lower levels of irisin (a hormone normally released by exercise) and an abnormal response when it was present, blocked by another protein called thrombospondin-1 — pointing to a new molecular explanation for why any exertion triggers a crash.

_(Full text in `paper.txt`; the summary above is the curated plain-language abstract — deepen from the full text when reading.)_

## Why this matters

Identifies a previously unknown pathway behind post-exertional malaise and suggests thrombospondin-1 and its signalling partners as possible targets for drugs that reduce the crash after activity.
