## Summary

Studying symptom surveys from 748 German ME/CFS patients, the authors showed that groups of symptoms which tend to occur together (e.g. brain fog, sensory sensitivity, and sleep problems as one group) statistically line up with specific underlying biological processes, such as immune, nerve, or gut-related dysfunction.

## Why this matters

A German patient-cohort study (the APAV-ME/CFS study) supporting the idea that ME/CFS should be split into biological subgroups rather than treated as one uniform illness — relevant to designing more targeted future drug trials.
