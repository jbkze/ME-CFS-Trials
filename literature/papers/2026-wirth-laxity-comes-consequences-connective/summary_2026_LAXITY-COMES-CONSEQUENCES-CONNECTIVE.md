# Laxity Comes with Consequences: Connective Tissue Disorders and Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (ME/CFS)

## Related files
- [[literature/papers/2026-wirth-laxity-comes-consequences-connective/citation.bib]]

## Summary

Klaus Wirth proposes that loose, stretchy connective tissue (as seen in some joint-hypermobility conditions) and ME/CFS can make each other worse in a two-way cycle: low oxygen and overactive immune cells in ME/CFS may weaken the 'glue' that holds collagen fibres together, while pre-existing loose ligaments and an unstable neck can trigger the blood-flow and blood-pressure problems seen in ME/CFS.

## Why this matters

From Wirth (Mitodicure): extends his muscle/blood-flow theory of ME/CFS to connective tissue and joint hypermobility, a link some patients report — but this is an early, not-yet-peer-reviewed hypothesis paper (posted to a preprint server, not medRxiv/bioRxiv), and other researchers have publicly questioned some of its claims, so treat it as a working idea rather than established science.
