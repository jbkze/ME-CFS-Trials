# Human Herpesvirus-6 Reactivation, Mitochondrial Fragmentation, and the Coordination of Antiviral and Metabolic Phenotypes in Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome

## Related files
- [[literature/papers/2020-schreiner-human-herpesvirus-reactivation-mitochondrial/citation.bib]]

## Summary

Suggests that reactivation of a common herpesvirus (HHV-6) can shatter cells' energy factories (mitochondria), switching the body into a protective but energy-starved state.

## Why this matters

Connects a viral trigger to the energy failure seen in ME/CFS — a possible mechanism linking an old infection to lasting illness. Co-authored by Scheibenbogen, Naviaux and Prusty.
