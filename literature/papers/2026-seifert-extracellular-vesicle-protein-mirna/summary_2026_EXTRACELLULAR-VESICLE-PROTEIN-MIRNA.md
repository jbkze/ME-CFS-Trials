## Summary

Tiny particles shed by blood cells (extracellular vesicles) carry distinctive protein and genetic signals in ME/CFS patients, and lower levels of one small genetic molecule (microRNA let-7b-5p) were linked to worse fatigue and pain.

_(Full text in `paper.txt`; the summary above is the curated plain-language abstract — deepen from the full text when reading.)_

## Why this matters

From the Scheibenbogen group: these vesicle-based markers could become a blood test for ME/CFS and help separate patients into subgroups for more targeted treatment.
