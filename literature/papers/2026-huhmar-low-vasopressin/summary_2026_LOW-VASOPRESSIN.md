## Summary

Found that levels of vasopressin — a hormone that helps control water balance, blood flow, and stress responses — were lower in ME/CFS patients than in healthy people.

_(Full text in `paper.txt`; the summary above is the curated plain-language abstract — deepen from the full text when reading.)_

## Why this matters

Low vasopressin could help explain symptoms like dizziness, excessive thirst, and fatigue, and may become a diagnostic marker or a target for treatment.
