## Summary

Proposes how even light activity can trigger a 'crash' (the delayed worsening of symptoms after exertion, called post-exertional malaise): the cells' energy units falter, this sparks inflammation, and the inflammation reaches the brain and keeps the cycle going.

## Why this matters

Offers a clear, testable explanation for ME/CFS's defining symptom — crashing after exertion — and points toward energy- and inflammation-based treatments.
